# Omnis Salestrack - User Manual

## 1. Introduction
Welcome to **Omnis Salestrack**. This system is your central hub for managing machine orders, sales quotations, and customer enquiries. It is designed to give you a real-time view of your sales pipeline and help you keep customers informed.

---

## 2. Getting Started

### Logging In
1.  Launch the **Omnis Desktop** application.
2.  Enter your **Username** and **Password**.
3.  Click **Login**.
    *   *Note*: The system will automatically connect you to Salestrack. If you have access to other systems (like Fleetrack), they will be available separately.

### Navigation Sidebar
The bar on the left is your main navigation tool. Click any icon to switch views:
- **Dashboard**: Your home screen with live statistics.
- **Orders (FMB)**: The list of all current machine orders.
- **Quotations**: Your history of quotes and the place to create new ones.
- **Customer Enquiries**: Track new leads and opportunities.
- **Products**: Browse the item catalog.
- **Customers**: View your client database.

---

## 3. Dashboard
The Dashboard gives you an instant "Health Check" of the business.

### KPI Cards
The top row shows colored cards with key numbers:
- **Orders**: Total active orders.
- **Pipeline**: Potential value of open quotes.
- **Enquiries**: New leads needing attention.

### "At Risk" Alerts
- If a card turns **Red** or flashes, it requires immediate attention (e.g., an Order that is overdue).
- Click on any card to jump to the relevant list.

---

## 4. Orders (FMB) Management
This is where you track machines from order to handover.

### Viewing Orders
The list shows all active orders. Key columns to watch:
- **Status**: Shows the current stage (e.g., "Pending", "In Transit").
- **Days Left**:
    - **Green**: On track.
    - **Orange**: Due within 5 days.
    - **Red (⚠️)**: Overdue.

### Filtering the List
Use the **Filter Bar** at the top-right to find specific orders:
- **Search Text**: Type a Customer Name or Machine Model.
- **ORDER Date**: specific date the order was placed.
- **HANDOVER Date**: specific date the machine is due.
- **Reset**: Click this button to clear all filters and show everything.

### Updating Orders (Inline Editing)
You can update order details directly in the list without opening a new page.
1.  **Edit Notes**: Double-click the **Notes** column. A text box will appear. Type your update.
2.  **Edit Date**: Double-click the **Revised Handover** column to pick a new date.
3.  **Saving**:
    - Click anywhere outside the box to save.
    - For Notes, you can also press **Ctrl + Enter**.

### Sending Customer Updates
When you update a Note or Revised Date, the system silently saves your change and adds it to your **Notification Center**.
1.  **Bell Icon**: Look for the **Bell** icon in the top-right corner. A red badge will show the number of pending updates.
2.  **Reviewing Updates**: Click the bell to see a list of all changes.
     - *Note*: If you make multiple changes to the same order (e.g., Note AND Date), they will be grouped into a single notification.
3.  **Action**:
    - **Preview**: Click "Preview" to see the message for a specific order.
    - **Send**: Click "Send" to notify that customer immediately.
    - **Send All**: Use the "Send All Updates" button to clear the list and notify everyone at once.
    - *Note*: You can clear the list without sending by clicking "Clear".

---

## 5. Quotations
Manage your sales quotes here.

- **Viewing**: The list shows Quote Number, Customer, and Total Value.
- **Creating a Quote**:
    1.  Click the **+ New Quotation** button (top right).
    2.  Select a **Customer**.
    3.  Add **Lines** (Machines or Parts).
    4.  Click **Save**.

---

## 6. Customer Enquiries
Use this section to log new leads or phone calls.
- **Open**: Leads that are still being worked on.
- **Closed**: Leads that have been converted to Quotes or orders.

---

## 7. Reference Data
- **Products**: Use the search bar to find Item Codes and Descriptions.
- **Customers**: Look up contact details for your clients.
- **Group Sales**: A read-only view of historical sales data for reporting.
